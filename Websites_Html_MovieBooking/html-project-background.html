Today's Movie Spotlight

This is a 2019 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics superhero team - the Avengers, produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. In the film, following the devastating events of Avengers: Infinity War, a grave course of events was set in motion by the Mad Titan - Thanos. The universe is in ruins, half of the universe has been wiped out and the Avengers' ranks has been fractured. This compels the remaining Avengers and their allies to take one final stand once more to undo Thano's decimation and hopefully restore order to the universe once and for all, no matter what consequences may be in store, in this Marvel Studios’ grand conclusion to twenty-two films, Avengers: Endgame.

Top Facts About The Movie

Top Facts About The Movie

  1. Filming began in August 2017 at Pinewood Atlanta Studios in Fayette County, Georgia, shooting back-to-back with Infinity War, and ended in January 2018. Additional filming took place in the Metro and Downtown Atlanta areas, New York, Scotland, and England.
  2. The film was announced in October 2014 as Avengers: Infinity War – Part 2, but Marvel later removed this title.The official title was revealed in December 2018.
  3. With an estimated budget of $356 million, it is one of the most expensive films ever made. It grossed $858.4 million in the United States and Canada, and $1.939 billion in other territories, for a worldwide total of $2.8 billion.[3] It is the highest-grossing film of all time,The film had a worldwide opening of $1.2 billion, the biggest of all time
  4. The marketing campaign for Endgame cost over $200 million, the most for any Marvel Studios film,headed by Dustin Sandoval, vice president of digital marketing for Marvel Studios
  5. Alan Silvestri composed the film's score in June 2016.
  6. Endgame had its world premiere at the Los Angeles Convention Center on April 22, 2019.[140][141] Disney converted the convention center's Hall K for the film's premiere, working with Dolby and QSC Audio to install a 70 foot (21 metres) screen, Dolby Vision projectors, and a Dolby Atmos sound system
  7. was released in the US on digital download by Walt Disney Studios Home Entertainment on July 30, and on Ultra HD Blu-ray, Blu-ray, and DVD on August 13
